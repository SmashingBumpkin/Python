\n\nSpicy Egg Fried Rice\n\nIngredients:\n\n- 2 cups cooked basmati rice\n- 2 tablespoons olive oil\n- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil\n- 1/2 teaspoon black mustard seeds\n- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin\n- 1/4 teaspoon turmeric\n- 1/4 teaspoon chilli peppers (or more to taste)\n- 2 cloves garlic, minced\n- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper\n- 1/4 teaspoon garam masala\n- 1/2 teaspoon paprika\n- 2 tablespoons pine nuts\n- 2 eggs, lightly beaten\n- 2 tablespoons butter\n- 2 tablespoons milk\n- 2 tablespoons sesame seeds\n\nInstructions:\n\n1. Heat the olive oil and vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.\n\n2. Add the mustard seeds and cook until they begin to splutter, about 2 minutes.\n\n3. Add the cumin, turmeric, chilli peppers, garlic and black pepper and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.\n\n4. Add the cooked basmati rice, garam masala and paprika and stir to combine.\n\n5. Add the pine nuts and cook for 2 minutes.\n\n6. Push the rice mixture to the sides of the pan and pour in the beaten eggs. Stir to scramble the eggs.\n\n7. Once the eggs are cooked, stir to combine with the rice mixture.\n\n8. Add the butter and milk and stir to combine.\n\n9. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve. Enjoy!
\n\n\nSpicy Penne with Basmati Rice\n\nIngredients:\n\n- 2 tablespoons olive oil\n- 2 cloves garlic, minced\n- 1 onion, diced\n- 1 teaspoon black mustard seeds\n- 1 teaspoon ground cumin\n- 1 teaspoon dried basil\n- 1 teaspoon paprika\n- 1 teaspoon garam masala\n- 1 teaspoon turmeric\n- 1 teaspoon black pepper\n- 1 teaspoon chilli peppers, chopped\n- 1 teaspoon rosemary\n- 2 tablespoons sesame seeds\n- 2 tablespoons pine nuts\n- 2 cups penne pasta\n- 2 cups basmati rice\n- 2 cups vegetable stock\n- 2 cubes stock cubes\n- 2 tablespoons pau bhaji\n- 2 tablespoons butter\n- 1/4 cup milk\n\nInstructions:\n\n1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.\n\n2. Add the garlic and onion and cook until the onion is softened, about 5 minutes.\n\n3. Add the mustard seeds, cumin, basil, paprika, garam masala, turmeric, black pepper, chilli peppers, rosemary, sesame seeds, and pine nuts and cook for another 2 minutes.\n\n4. Add the penne pasta and basmati rice and stir to combine.\n\n5. Add the vegetable stock, stock cubes, pau bhaji, butter, and milk and bring to a boil.\n\n6. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.\n\n7. Serve hot. Enjoy!
\n\nPenne Alfredo with Black Mustard Seeds\n\nIngredients:\n\n- 1 package penne pasta\n- 2 tablespoons olive oil\n- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil\n- 2 tablespoons black mustard seeds\n- 2 cloves garlic, minced\n- 2 tablespoons ground cumin\n- 1 tablespoon dried basil\n- 1 tablespoon garam masala\n- 1 tablespoon paprika\n- 1 teaspoon turmeric\n- 1 teaspoon black pepper\n- 1 cup cooked basmati rice\n- 2 tablespoons pau bhaji\n- 1/4 cup pine nuts\n- 1/4 cup sesame seeds\n- 1/2 cup milk\n- 2 tablespoons butter\n- 2 eggs\n- 1 tablespoon rosemary, chopped\n\nInstructions:\n\n1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the penne according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.\n\n2. Heat the olive oil and vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds and cook until they start to pop, about 1 minute.\n\n3. Add the garlic, cumin, basil, garam masala, paprika, and turmeric and cook for another minute.\n\n4. Add the rice and pau bhaji and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.\n\n5. Add the pine nuts, sesame seeds, milk, butter, and eggs and cook until the eggs are cooked through, about 4 minutes.\n\n6. Add the penne and rosemary and toss to combine.\n\n7. Serve warm, topped with additional sesame seeds, if desired.
